Insights

Insights | Interview with Dr. Tilly, Chief Data Scientist, Barentsz DataLab

"Five Levers Universities Can Pull to Strengthen Competitiveness"

1. Evidence-Based Research Investment

"Channel funds toward disciplines where marginal gains in citation impact are highest. Our modelling shows that targeted seed grants in emerging fields—rather than blanket increases—yield the greatest boost to Research scores."

2. Employer-Aligned Curriculum Design

"Map programme learning outcomes to real-time labour-market signals. Universities that embed industry capstones and micro-credentials see faster growth in Employment metrics."

3. Reputation Through Thought Leadership

"Global perception moves when faculty publish in open-access venues and lead international consortia. Visibility, not just volume, shifts Reputation."

4. Strategic International Partnerships

"Joint degree frameworks and co-authored papers with top-50 partners disproportionately lift the International indicator. Quality beats sheer head-count of exchange agreements."

5. Data-Driven Student Support

"Predictive analytics on retention and wellbeing correlates with downstream alumni success—closing the loop between student experience and graduate outcomes."

Dr. Tilly concludes: "Competitiveness is no longer a by-product of tradition; it is the result of disciplined, data-guided choices. Institutions that operationalise these levers tend to climb—and stay—upward in our ranking."
